Governance Stress-Tests

A hub for the Governance Stress-Test method: the checklist, worked example, and protocol note.

Governance Stress-Tests are a method for evaluating whether a public institution is ready to operate a technology it is about to adopt.

The method begins with a concrete decision: a procurement, pilot, model update, digital public infrastructure rollout, compute subsidy, vendor renewal, or deployment threshold. It asks whether the institution can evaluate the system, retain authority, inspect evidence, provide recourse, learn from failure, and revise or stop the deployment before the technology becomes routine administration.

Impact assessments usually test the system. Governance Stress-Tests test the institution around it.

The output is a Revised Governance Decision: the conditions that must exist before the technology can be responsibly used.

The Six Stages

StageWhat It Tests
1. Decision DefinitionWhether the decision, operator, affected parties, and claimed benefit are clear
2. Capability And ControlWhether the institution can evaluate, constrain, and learn from the system
3. Authority And ResponsibilityWhether legal authority, operational control, and technical knowledge align
4. Recourse And LearningWhether affected people can contest outcomes and the institution can learn from failures
5. Public-Value ConstraintsWhether opacity and delegated authority are tolerable at the risk level
6. Deployment ThresholdsWhether enough evidence and capacity exist before pilot, deployment, scale-up, or renewal

Version 1.0
